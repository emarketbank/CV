Meras wasn't built to be the biggest — but it grew

And that success is exactly what's creating today's challenge

Growth happened fast. Branches opened. Brands expanded. The scale became real.

This is the stage every healthcare group dreams of reaching. But it always comes with a question that isn't easy to answer:

How do we keep growing without losing clarity?

From the outside, it's clear that Meras isn't a typical clinic chain. It's an entity built across different phases—and each phase left its mark:

Brands built at different times. Branches opened under different circumstances. Patient experiences shaped by whatever growth stage was happening.

Nothing wrong with that. It's just an honest description of real growth.

The real challenge isn't opening more.
It isn't spending more.

The challenge is shifting from effort-based growth to understanding-based growth.

From

"Try and Win"

To

"Understand and Repeat"

Today, Meras is closer to a healthcare operating platform than a clinic group.

A platform managing patient experience, operations, physicians, multiple brands, and large-scale growth—all at once.

This demands a different kind of marketing. One that doesn't just drive demand—but creates clarity, structure, and direction.

In 2026, marketing's role at Meras isn't launching more campaigns.

It's building a foundation that makes every future campaign smarter, cheaper, and easier to measure.

2026 Objectives

Three Things We're Building

01

Understand the Full Patient Journey

From the first time someone hears about Meras—to booking, visiting, returning, and recommending.

  • Not at the advertising level
  • At the actual experience level
  • Where do people continue? Where do they drop off? Why?
02

Define Brand Roles Clearly

Without breaking the group's unified image.

  • Not every brand should speak the same language
  • Or target the same audience
  • Or sell the same way
  • Diversity is strength—when it's intentional
03

Turn Data Into Operational Decisions

Not complex dashboards. Clear visibility.

  • Who's growing right
  • Who needs intervention
  • Where to invest
